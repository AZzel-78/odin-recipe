CARBONARA


Ingredients

  • Rigato
  • Guanciale
  • Eggs
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Black Pepper
  • Salt

Cooking Steps

  1. Boil the Pasta

    2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop in your pasta and cook it until it is al dente (about 1-2 minutes less than the package instructions). Do not drain the water yet.

  2. Crisp the Meat

    3. While the pasta cooks, heat a large pan over medium heat (no extra oil needed). Add the guanciale or pancetta and fry until the fat renders and the meat becomes crispy and golden brown. Turn off the heat, but leave the meat and its rendered fat in the pan.

  3. Mix the Egg-Cheese Sauce

    4. In a medium bowl, whisk together the 2 whole eggs, 1 egg yolk, grated cheese, and a generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper until it forms a thick paste.

  4. Combine Pasta and Meat

    5. Using tongs or a spider skimmer, transfer the hot pasta directly from the boiling water into the pan with the crispy meat and fat. Toss everything together for a minute over very low heat so the pasta absorbs the flavors.

  5. The Magic Step (Creating the Sauce)

    6. Remove the pan entirely from the heat source. This is crucial so you don't scramble the eggs. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Immediately begin tossing vigorously and stirring. Ladle in about 1/4 cup of the hot pasta water. The residual heat from the pasta and the pan, combined with the starchy water, will melt the cheese and emulsify the eggs into a smooth, creamy sauce.
