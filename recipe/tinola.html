ADOBO


Adobo is the first dish that Francis liked because her grandmother is great at cooking it. It's sweet and the chicken meat is tender everytime her grandma cooks it


Ingredients

  • Chicken - mostly used part of the chicken is thighs.
  • Pepper and Salt
  • Green Papaya
  • Water
  • Cooking Oil
  • Yellow Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Fish Sauce

Cooking Steps

  1. Sauté the Aromatics

    2. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the ginger, garlic, and onions until the onions are translucent and the ginger smells incredibly fragrant (about 2-3 minutes).

  2. Sear the Chicken

    3. Add the chicken pieces to the pot. Cook for about 3-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is lightly browned on the outside.

  3. Season and Simmer

    4. Pour in the fish sauce (patis) and stir well to coat the chicken. Let it cook for another minute to lock in the flavor. Pour in the rice washing or water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Cover and let it cook for 15-20 minutes until the chicken is tender.

  4. Add the Vegetables

    5. Add the green papaya or chayote wedges. Cover and simmer for another 5-8 minutes, or until the vegetables are fork-tender but not mushy.

  5. Final Touch

    6. Taste the broth and season with salt and black pepper as needed. Turn off the heat and stir in the hot pepper leaves or malunggay. Cover the pot and let the residual heat wilt the greens for about 1-2 minutes.
